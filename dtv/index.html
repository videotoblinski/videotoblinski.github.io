A History of Digital Television

Television came around in the 1920s in a crude mechanical form. However, as time went on, the television was refined and by the 1950s became one of the most popular forms of mass media. In the '60s, color television became readily available, and in the never-ending quest to make television look better, some nerds over in Japan working for NHK dropped the first real HD television standard in the mid-'80s, known as MUSE Hi-Vision. This led to the first digital television stations signing on in the late '90s and early 2000s, which most governments saw as an opportunity to free up some analog channels. So, they axed analog over the course of several years and made digital the preferred method of watching television.

The digital television transition, specifically in the United States, has always been an interesting topic to me, so I've decided to throw a website together about it.
